What is the NBA?

The NBA or National Basketball Association- is known as one of the
best sports in the world, American professional basketball league (NBA).
The NBA was founded in New York on June 6, 1946 as the Basketball
Association of America. It adopted its current name in 1949, after
the union of several clubs from the defunct National Basketball League.
The opening game was a New York Knicks - Toronto Huskies, on November 1,
1946. The first game was seen by 7,090 spectators.

REGULAR SEASON

In the regular season each team plays 82 games, divided equally between home
and away games. The calendar is not the same for everyone. Teams meet
opponents from their own division four times; before those of the other
two divisions of his conference, three or four times; and against those
of the other conference, twice a year.29 Therefore, a team can have an
easy or difficult schedule, depending on the division or conference in
which it is located. After recent NHL schedule format changes, basketball
is the only top-tier American league where all franchises play each other
during the regular season, and where a season ticket guarantees you being
able to see your team every time they come to town to play during the regular
season.

POSTSEASON

The NBA Playoffs consist of four rounds of competition between sixteen
teams divided into the Western Conference and the Eastern Conference,
eight teams from each Conference. The winners of the First Round
(or conference quarterfinals) advance to the Conference Semifinals,
then to the Conference Finals, and the winners to the NBA Finals, played
between the champions of each conference.

FINALS

In the final round, the champions of each conference meet in the best
of seven games. The first to win in four games will be known as the
NBA champion. It is contested annually in June, and the champion has
been awarded the Larry O'Brien Championship Trophy since 1977, when it
replaced the Walter A. Brown Trophy.34 The idea of calling the Larry
O'Brien Championship Trophy, is in honor of David Stern's predecessor NBA
commissioner, Larry O'Brien. Each player on the winning team, as well as
the coach and general manager, is awarded a champion's ring.
